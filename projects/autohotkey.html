AutoHotkey

AutoHotkey is automation software, you can search for it and go download it right now for free. It does a LOT, but I think the main uses are setting up custom hotkeys and automating mouse/keyboard inputs.


I LOVE AutoHotkey. I started using it at AT&T and I've used it everywhere I could since.


Click on images below to see full size and see more info.


The Beginning

My use of AutoHotkey was born out of necessity. At AT&T, they needed to generate hundreds of Apple IDs to attach to iPads and send to customers. They had everyone in the office start making them manually, so a co-worker and myself thought, there must be an easier way.

AutoHotkey was the solution, it simulated the clicking and typing throughout the process.

It started out very clunky and unreliable, but got better over time. Through multiple iterations, we went from the whole office making them -- to hiring a few temps to monitor the script -- to letting it run solo.

Soon...

I kept playing around with AutoHotkey to see what it could do. I ended up making a pseudo-ticketing system to do our job. At the time, we got phone calls, took down info, and sent it to our warehouse for replacement devices. The info was different for each customer. I create a GUI with templates for each customer to speed the whole thing up and cut down on errors.

Our whole team ended up using it.

Our job responsibilities became more involved, but I ended up making a tool for that too, so creating a troubleshooting ticket wasn't a daunting task.

Have a look:

This example shows a ticket being created in the AT&T ticketing system, AOTS (left) by my AutoHotkey script (right). Instead of filling out every field manually, you can choose a customer, issue type, fill in some basic info and click 'submit'. AutoHotkey fills out the ticket for you in AOTS faster than any person could, preventing any manual entry errors.

Now

I use AutoHotkey on my home computer. I also used it at Linn County. I will use it anywhere I'm allowed to.

Things I've used it for:

  • Downloading hundreds of iPad logs (had to be done individually) and naming them appropiately (all had the same name normally)
  • Automating Outlook, Excel, Word
  • Several tools to make programming easier
  • Quick program launcher on my PC
  • A tool to search a phrase on various sites/applications
  • Changed capslock into a modifier key like ctrl and alt with custom hotkeys
  • I made my own version of 'Cards Against Humanity' (why not?)
