ePulse and Poll Pads

ePulse and Poll Pads are products of Knowink.

ePulse is the server. Poll Pads are iPads set up with a Poll Pad app for checking in voters.

I managed the configuration and maintenance of both at Linn County. This was (and is) used on election day, for early voting, and during training for election workers.

Along with the configuration, I also used Mobile Device Management (MDM) to configure the iPads themselves using Cisco Meraki software.

Finally, the iPads were connected to a Verizon hot spot to communicate with our office during elections. I also configured these using their management platform.


ePulse

I managed the configuration of the server. This included assigning devices to locations, custom screens to guide workers, and custom receipt templates.

We also used this to monitor the iPads on election day and we could see how many voters are checking in.

Poll Pads

We had over 300 iPads to manage. They were used for election worker training and election day, among other things.

Cisco Meraki

Meraki was used to push out iPad settings and perform bulk actions on iPads, like updating the OS.

Verizon Hotspots

Each location has a hotspot so the iPads can communicate with our office. These were set up using Verizon MDM.
