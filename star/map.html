Utilizing Existing Tools:
Mapping Election Workers

Situation

Assigning hundreds of workers to about 80 polling locations was always a monumental task. We had several tools to assist with the process, including a Microsoft Access database for the actual assignments, which showed mileage based on Google Maps API once someone was assigned to a location. This was nice, but it didn't help me actually assign the workers.

Task

I needed a way to make intelligent decisions when assigning workers. I had to take into consideration where my available workers were, so I didn't have people going across the county. We paid mileage, and at 6 AM and 10 PM, travelling that far could be dangerous.

Action

I utilized Google Maps along with exports from our database. This allowed me to visualize where my available workers were and where I needed people.

I made several SQL queries for different situations, like locations that needed Democrats or Republicans (locations legally needed to be party balanced). I had exports that had my workers and their parties, so I could color code them based on parties. Putting them on a map of Linn County allowed me to take the river into consideration, and find the best person for the location, not just someone who is close.

Result

This undoubtedly saved tax-payer dollars on mileage spent for poll workers. It also made the poll workers' lives easier, as I didn't have them travelling across the county after a 16 hour day.
