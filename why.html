My History

...in Elections

I came into elections with no prior election experience. I started as a contractor focusing on equipment prep for several months, before being hired on as a permanent employee. My job was geared more towards working with election official volunteers: assigning to locations, training, and paying them. I had to learn quickly without much guidance, as my predecessor was not the type to leave documentation.

I quickly acclimated to my position and made it my own, using technology to solve the complex problems with the job. I also continued responsibilities with the technology until that became my full-time job (a newly created job, based on work I was doing). My goal during much of this time was modernizing, standardizing, and documenting procedures, eliminating the need for institutional knowledge.

...at AT&T

Before elections, I worked at AT&T Mobility Solutions. I started as a contractor before being hired on full time to support ruggedized devices over the phone for a handful of companies. During my time there, our department shifted from that to Mobile Device Management support. We supported a much larger number of admins and end user's to manage their MDM solutions.

My focus at AT&T became automation and documentation. I became the subject matter expert (SME) for one MDM solution we supported and worked with the other SME to document troubleshooting procedures. I created software solutions to speed up and standardize ticket creation along with setting up custom Apple IDs for customers.

...and the rest

Earlier jobs were over-the-phone tech support jobs. I supported network equipment for hotels at Ovation Networks. Before that, I remotely installed and supported anti-virus software at Security Coverage as a student intern.
